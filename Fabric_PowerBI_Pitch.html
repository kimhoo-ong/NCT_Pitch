02 · THE CHALLENGE
03 / 15
Where the 1–3 days actually go

The challenge today.

Every new project starts from zero

Copy last project's Excel, find-and-replace assumptions, hope nothing breaks. Setup alone takes hours.

One variable change = long wait

Adjust selling price by 5% — wait for hundreds of formulas across 10+ sheets to recalculate.

What-if questions are slow

Director asks 'what if we delay sales by 6 months?' — answering live is impossible.

Three scenarios = three files

Bear, Base, Optimistic each lives in a separate workbook. Comparing them needs manual consolidation.

Historical projects don't help

Past project data sits in old folders. No way to pull comparable assumptions automatically.

Outputs are slides, not data

Every report is a hand-built PPT. Once finalised, the underlying numbers are frozen and stale.

05 · BUILT FOR COLLABORATION

Three roles, one platform — different views, same data

Built for collaboration.

QS
Quantity Surveyor
Inputs and assumptions
What they do
  • Sets construction cost per psf
  • Updates unit mix and sizing
  • Maintains material cost benchmarks
  • Adjusts contingency factors
IC
Investment Committee
Scenario evaluation
What they do
  • Reviews GDV / margin / cost structure
  • Compares Bear / Base / Optimistic
  • Stress-tests sensitivity
  • Approves or vetoes the project
F
Finance Team
Cash flow and funding
What they do
  • Models monthly cash flow
  • Identifies peak funding gap
  • Plans equity / debt mix
  • Tracks actual vs forecast
Single source of truth — when QS updates a cost, IC sees the new margin. When sales velocity changes, the funding profile updates.
